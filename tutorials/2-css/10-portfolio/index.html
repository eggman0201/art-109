About Me

Born and raised in San Jose, Emily Tran's always been close to home. More specifically, she was close to the laptop (family couldn't afford a PC), where she spent her formative years. It didn't take long before she graduated from simple flash games to social media, where she was exposed to the breadth of what the world wide web had to offer, for better or for worse.

Instead of pretending like she isn't chronically online, Emily embraces the digital, leaning on cutesy Harajuku-inspired aesthetics. Her work draws attention to the raster, with personal works often using brushes that don't have anti-aliasing. To her, obvious pixelation is like impasto on a canvas; it draws attention to the medium, adding context and texture to a piece.

Furthermore, she wouldn't be able to hide her years of being online even if she tried; her entire knowledge base is random shit she found online. She, like many others who learned how to bypass school censors before they learned derivatives, and she is savvy to internet trends, cultures, and countercultures. Ask her anything about what's currently happening online, and she can probably spew out the Sparknotes on a hot topic, names, places, and random fun facts she learned through learning more about a Hot Take against her will.

When she's trying (and failing) to not doomscroll, Emily enjoys playing video games, drawing, making jewelry, working out, or crocheting (all of which she learned to do through the internet). The fun part of having access to billions of peoples' every thoughts and whims (give or take some bots) is learning all sorts of new hobbies and gizmos to try out. The internet taketh and taketh, but sometimes, it giveth(s??) a morsal of something cool to try out.
